Home Calisthenics Routine (WFH Micro + Intensive) — APT + Fascia

Built for: home workouts, no equipment (mat only) + focus on anterior pelvic tilt (deep core + glutes + hip flexors) and adding Jason van Blerk fascia as an option.

WFH: 10 × 5-min blocks Not-home: 2 intensive sessions APT/Fascia notes

WFH Days — 10 Micro Workouts (≈5 minutes each)

Pick 4–10 blocks across the day. Each block has a player already loaded. Use Play to swap the video for a specific move.

Micro 1 — APT Reset: Breathing + Pelvic Control

Goal: ribs down, posterior pelvic tilt control, deep core.

5 min

  1. 90/90 Breathing

    ~60–90s: slow nasal inhale, long exhale. Keep ribs “down”.

  2. Supine Posterior Pelvic Tilt

    2×10 reps: flatten low-back to mat, then relax.

  3. Dead Bug (slow)

    1–2 min: keep low-back from arching. Move slow.

Now showing: 90/90 Breathing

Micro 2 — Hip Flexor / Quad Release (APT helper)

Goal: open the front of hips so pelvis can return neutral.

5 min

  1. Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

    2×45s/side: squeeze glute on back leg, tuck pelvis slightly.

  2. Couch Stretch

    45–60s/side: don’t crank low-back; keep glute engaged.

Now showing: Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

Micro 3 — Glute Wake-Up (anti-APT)

Goal: get glutes online so hips stop “dumping forward”.

5 min

  1. Glute Bridge

    2×12 reps: drive through heels, don’t over-arch at the top.

  2. Hip Hinge Drill

    1–2 min: push hips back, neutral spine, feel hamstrings.

Now showing: Glute Bridge

Micro 4 — Core: Anti-Extension (stops low-back arching)

Goal: train “ribs down” + pelvis control under tension.

5 min

  1. Bent-Knee Hollow Hold

    3×20–30s: flatten low-back, breathe, don’t strain neck.

  2. Forearm Plank

    2×30–45s: squeeze glutes, ribs down, long spine.

Now showing: Bent-Knee Hollow Hold

Micro 5 — Posture Upper-Back (desk-friendly)

Goal: strengthen upper back / scap control (helps overall alignment).

5 min

  1. Prone Y-T-W Raises

    2 rounds: 6–8 reps each letter. Slow + controlled.

  2. Reverse Snow Angels

    10–15 reps: keep chest down, move from shoulders.

  3. Scapular Push-Ups

    10–15 reps: arms straight, only shoulder blades move.

Now showing: Prone Y-T-W Raises

Micro 6 — Spine & Thoracic Opener (tight from sitting)

Goal: restore rotation + flex/extend without dumping into low-back.

5 min

  1. Cat-Cow

    60–90s: move segment by segment, breathe with motion.

  2. Open Book (Thoracic Rotation)

    6–8 reps/side: rotate from upper back, keep knees stacked.

  3. Standing Trunk Rotations

    45–60s: gentle rotations, don’t crank low-back.

Now showing: Cat-Cow

Micro 7 — Quick Warm-Up Reset (shoulders + hips)

Goal: circulation + loosen stiffness without “workout mode”.

5 min

  1. Arm Swings (dynamic warm-up)

    60s: relaxed, smooth, open chest.

  2. Hip Hinge Drill

    60–90s: hips back, neutral spine.

Now showing: Arm Swings

Micro 8 — Lower Body Strength Mini

Goal: legs + glutes + posture-friendly strength.

5 min

  1. Bodyweight Squat

    2×10–15 reps: brace core, sit between heels, stand tall.

  2. Reverse Lunge

    2×8–10/side: step back, torso tall, drive through front heel.

Now showing: Bodyweight Squat

Micro 9 — Upper Body Push Mini

Goal: chest + shoulders + core tension.

5 min

  1. Push-Up

    2×AMRAP with good form (or knee/incline if needed).

  2. Pike Push-Up

    2×6–12 reps: shoulders work; keep control, no neck strain.

Not-Home Days — 2 Intensive Workouts

These are longer and harder. Still no equipment needed.

Intensive A — Strength + APT Focus (35–45 min)

Warm-up → Strength Circuit → Core → Cooldown.

35–45 min

  1. Warm-up: Morning Fascia Reset (pick 5–8 min)

    Then a few deep breaths; don’t rush.

  2. Bodyweight Squat

    3 rounds × 10–20 reps.

  3. Bulgarian Split Squat

    3 rounds × 8–12/side.

  4. Push-Up

    3 rounds × 6–15 reps (or knee/incline).

  5. Pike Push-Up

    2–3 rounds × 6–12 reps.

  6. Glute Bridge

    2–3 rounds × 12–20 reps.

  7. Dead Bug + Side Plank (core)

    Dead Bug 60s + Side Plank 30–45s/side (2 rounds).

  8. Cooldown: Couch Stretch

    45–60s/side.

Now showing: Morning Fascia Reset

Intensive B — Conditioning + Core (30–40 min)

4–6 rounds: keep form, keep moving, short rests.

30–40 min

  1. Mountain Climbers

    30–45s per round.

  2. No-Push-Up Burpee

    8–12 reps per round.

  3. Bodyweight Squat

    12–20 reps per round.

  4. Reverse Lunge

    8–12/side per round.

  5. Bent-Knee Hollow Hold

    20–40s per round.

  6. Optional finisher: Wall Sit

    60–120s at end (or 2×60s).

Now showing: Mountain Climbers

If you ever feel low-back “pinching” during core work: shorten range, slow down, and switch back to Micro 1 for a day.

APT + “Fascia” Notes (simple + practical)

Anterior pelvic tilt is usually improved by combining: deep core control (posterior tilt, dead bug, hollow holds), glute strength (glute bridges, squats, split squats), and hip flexor/quad mobility (half-kneeling + couch stretch).

Fascia routines can be a great “reset” for stiffness/stress. Treat them like a nervous system downshift + mobility, and keep the strength work as the main driver of posture change.
