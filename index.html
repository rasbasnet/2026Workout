Home Calisthenics Routine (WFH Micro + Intensive) — APT + Fascia

Built for: home workouts, no equipment (mat only) + focus on anterior pelvic tilt (deep core + glutes + hip flexors) and adding Jason van Blerk fascia as an option.

WFH: 10 × 5-min blocks Not-home: 2 intensive sessions APT/Fascia notes

WFH Days — 10 Micro Workouts (Morning → Night, ≈5 minutes each)

Do these in order from morning to night. Balanced across upper, lower, APT (core/glutes/hip flexors), and fascia/reset. Tap Play to load a form video into that block’s player.

Micro 1 (Morning) — Wake-Up: Mobility + Breath

Loosen spine/hips and set a neutral ribs-over-pelvis position.

5 min

  1. Cat-Cow

    60s: move slowly, breathe with the motion.

  2. Open Book (Thoracic Rotation)

    30–45s/side: rotate from upper back, keep knees stacked.

  3. Standing Trunk Rotations

    45–60s: gentle rotation, don’t crank low-back.

  4. 90/90 Breathing

    4–6 slow breaths: long exhales, ribs “down”.

Micro 2 (AM) — APT Reset: Pelvic Control + Deep Core

Stop low-back arching early: ribs down, pelvis neutral.

5 min

  1. Supine Posterior Pelvic Tilt

    2×10 reps: flatten low-back to mat, then relax.

  2. Dead Bug (slow)

    6–8/side: keep low-back from lifting.

  3. Bent-Knee Hollow Hold

    2×20–30s: breathe, don’t strain neck.

Now showing: Supine Posterior Pelvic Tilt

Micro 3 (Late AM) — Hip Flexor + Quad Opener (APT helper)

Mobility to counter long sitting; keep glute engaged on the back leg.

5 min

  1. Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

    45s/side: slight pelvic tuck, squeeze back glute.

  2. Couch Stretch

    45s/side: don’t over-arch, keep ribs down.

  3. Glute Bridge (activation)

    10–15 reps: 1s pause at the top, no low-back crank.

Now showing: Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

Micro 4 (Midday) — Upper Body: Push Strength + Scap Control

Balances the day’s upper body volume (chest/shoulders) without equipment.

5 min

  1. Push-Up

    2 sets: 6–15 reps (knee/incline is fine).

  2. Pike Push-Up

    2 sets: 6–12 reps (shoulders). Control tempo.

  3. Scapular Push-Ups

    1–2 sets: 10–15 reps (arms straight; move shoulder blades).

Now showing: Push-Up

Micro 5 (Early PM) — Lower Body Strength (Squat/Lunge)

Leg strength + extra calorie burn; keep torso tall and braced.

5 min

  1. Bodyweight Squat

    12–20 reps: brace core, stand tall.

  2. Reverse Lunge

    8–12/side: drive through front heel.

  3. Wall Sit

    45–60s: keep ribs down and breathe.

Now showing: Bodyweight Squat

Micro 6 (Mid PM) — Upper Body: Back/Posture (Pull-Substitute)

No pulling equipment: strengthen upper back/rear delts and shoulder blades.

5 min

  1. Prone Y-T-W Raises

    6–8 reps each letter (2 rounds if time).

  2. Reverse Snow Angels

    10–15 reps: keep ribs down, slow control.

  3. Scapular Push-Ups

    10–15 reps: protract/retract only (arms straight).

Now showing: Prone Y-T-W Raises

Micro 7 (Late PM) — Glutes + Hamstrings (Anti-APT)

Posterior chain strength so hips stop tipping forward.

5 min

  1. Glute Bridge

    12–20 reps: pause at top, don’t over-arch.

  2. Single-Leg Glute Bridge

    6–12/side: keep pelvis level (slow tempo).

  3. Hip Hinge Drill

    10 slow reps: push hips back, neutral spine.

Now showing: Glute Bridge

Micro 8 (Evening) — Core Stability (Hold Neutral)

Anti-extension + anti-rotation: supports APT correction.

5 min

  1. Side Plank

    30–45s/side: keep ribs stacked, glutes lightly on.

  2. Bird Dog

    6–8/side: slow reach, no low-back arch.

  3. Forearm Plank

    30–45s: squeeze glutes, ribs down, breathe.

Not-home Days — 2 Intensive Sessions (≈35–45 minutes)

No equipment (mat only). No pulling. Balanced for upper push, legs, APT correction (core/glutes/hip flexors), plus fascia and posture work (Y-T-W / snow angels).

Intensive A — Strength + Posture + Finisher

Strength focus, APT-friendly warm-up, posture (pull-substitute), then a short sweat finisher.

35–45 min

  1. Warm-up: Jason fascia reset

    2–3 min (follow along, easy pace).

  2. 90/90 Breathing

    4–6 slow breaths (long exhales, ribs down).

  3. Supine Posterior Pelvic Tilt

    2×10 slow reps (flatten low-back then relax).

  4. Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

    45s/side (squeeze back glute; don’t arch).

  5. Glute Bridge (activation)

    12–15 reps (1s pause; no low-back crank).

  6. Strength Circuit (3 rounds)

    Rest 60–90s after each round.

  7. Push-Up

    8–15 reps (incline/knee OK).

  8. Pike Push-Up

    6–12 reps (shoulders; controlled).

  9. Bodyweight Squat

    10–15 reps (3s down).

  10. Reverse Lunge

    8–12/side (steady, heel pressure).

  11. Dead Bug

    8/side (slow; low-back stays down).

  12. Prone Y-T-W Raises (posture)

    6 each shape (smooth, controlled).

  13. Finisher (6–8 min)

    30s on / 30s easy × 6–8 rounds.

  14. Mountain Climbers

    30s work / 30s easy.

  15. No-Push-Up Burpee

    Alternate with climbers each round.

  16. Cool-down: Couch Stretch

    45s/side (don’t arch).

  17. Open Book (Thoracic Rotation)

    30–45s/side.

APT + “Fascia” Notes (simple + practical)

Anterior pelvic tilt is usually improved by combining: deep core control (posterior tilt, dead bug, hollow holds), glute strength (glute bridges, squats, split squats), and hip flexor/quad mobility (half-kneeling + couch stretch).

Fascia routines can be a great “reset” for stiffness/stress. Treat them like a nervous system downshift + mobility, and keep the strength work as the main driver of posture change.
